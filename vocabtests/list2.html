  • In some industries, machines are used to provide services for customers, but there are limitations, for example, their inability to answer questions.
  • Due to the limitations of the study, the researchers had to settle for less conclusive results.
  • Living in an apartment is fine, but it does have its limitations - for example, you don't have your own garden.
  • Despite her limitations as an actress, she was a great entertainer.
  • Many of them realize the deficiency and limitations of science as the only savior of mankind.
[C] a limiting rule or circumstance; a restriction 【限制】:
  • One limitation of the study is that it does not represent the world’s population; it is mostly based on data from people over 45 in the US.
  • A student visa has clear limitations regarding the maximum number of employment hours per week.
  • The major limitation of early record players was the short playing time of the records.
  • Now obviously, we don't want to place very many limitations on our freedom of speech.
[U] the action of limiting something 【限制】:
  • the limitation of nuclear weapons
  • They favor anything that involves limitation of government control.
  • But this limitation should at least prevent us from resorting to force and multiplying the number of victims.
With increasing crop yields, more parts of the world could be fed with agricultural products and countries could reduce famine and starvation.
starvation
noun
[U] suffering or death caused by hunger 【（常造成死亡的）饑餓，挨餓；餓死】:
  • Advancement in food production technology would save thousands from starvation.
  • 20 million people face starvation unless a vast emergency aid programme is launched.
  • The animals had died of starvation.
  • They pay starvation wages (= not enough money to live on).
  • Their remaining options were sleeping on the streets, prostitution, crime and starvation.
starve
verb
starve, starves, starving, starved, starved
[I/T] to (cause someone to) become very weak or die because there is not enough food to eat 【（使）挨餓；（使）餓死】:
  • Whole communities starved to death during the long drought.
  • From talking to former prisoners in the camps, an obvious conclusion is that they have been starved.
[T often passive] If you are starved of something necessary or good, you do not receive enough of it. 【（使）缺乏】
  • People starved of sleep start to lose their concentration and may hallucinate.
